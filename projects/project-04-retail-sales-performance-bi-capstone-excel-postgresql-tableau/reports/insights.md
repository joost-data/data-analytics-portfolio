# Insights (Project 04)

- Revenue and sales volume are concentrated: a smaller set of cities and stores account for a large share of total results, while many locations contribute relatively little.
- Product performance is uneven across the hierarchy: a limited set of product groups drive most revenue, which supports tighter focus in assortment and availability planning.
- Promotion fields appear frequently in the data and vary by product/store; this supports further analysis, but the current work treats promos as descriptive (not causal).
- A simple regression test suggests date alone is not a useful predictor of sales in this sample, so performance differences are likely driven by other factors (product mix, location, and promotional patterns).
